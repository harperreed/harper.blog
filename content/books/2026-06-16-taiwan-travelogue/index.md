Taiwan Travelogue is a fictional work that transports readers to May 1938, where we follow Aoyama Chizuko, a burgeoning novelist who sets sail from Nagasaki to Taiwan, only to find herself swept away not by the official duties of her invitation from the Japanese government, but by the vibrant life and authentic flavors of the island. Chizuko's yearning for genuine experiences leads her to Chizuru, a young Taiwanese woman who becomes her interpreter and a pivotal figure in her journey.

As Chizuko travels the scenic vistas of Taiwan, delighting in local delicacies such as braised pork rice and winter melon tea, her relationship with Chizuru evolves from professional to deeply personal, filled with charming exchanges and unspoken emotions. However, Chizuru's reservations introduce a rift that forces Chizuko to confront the complexities underlying their connection, illuminating the dynamics of power and intimacy shaped by their colonial realities.

Written by acclaimed author Yáng Shuāng-zǐ, the book first captured hearts in its original Mandarin Chinese publication in 2020, going on to win the prestigious Golden Tripod Award, Taiwan's highest literary recognition. It combines a rich narrative with a nuanced exploration of history and relationships, making it an important read for anyone interested in themes of identity, love, and the remnants of colonial legacies.
