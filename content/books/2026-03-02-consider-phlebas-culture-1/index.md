'Consider Phlebas' is the groundbreaking first novel of Iain M. Banks' renowned Culture series, released in 2009 by Orbit. Set against a sprawling backdrop of intergalactic conflict, the novel plunges readers into a universe where the Idirans—warriors driven by faith—clash with the Culture, a post-scarcity, utopian civilization governed by advanced AIs.

At the heart of this cosmic struggle is Horza, the Changer—an agent caught between ideals, tasked with retrieving a fugitive Mind hidden within an enigmatic labyrinth on a forsaken world. As Horza and his ragtag team of mercenaries, both human and machine, navigate perilous territories, they confront not only external enemies but the deeper moral dilemmas that arise during warfare.

Critically acclaimed, 'Consider Phlebas' has garnered an average rating of 3.85 on Goodreads, based on over 101,000 ratings, highlighting its impact within the science fiction canon. Readers appreciate Banks' masterful storytelling that combines high-stakes adventure with deep philosophical questions about identity, loyalty, and the essence of humanity.

Iain M. Banks (1954-2013) is celebrated for his contributions to speculative fiction, with a prolific body of work that explores the interplay of humanity, technology, and society through imaginative narratives.
