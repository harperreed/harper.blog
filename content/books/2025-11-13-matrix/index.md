In "Matrix", Lauren Groff brings us a gripping narrative set against the backdrop of history, featuring Marie de France, a young woman who is cast away from the royal court due to her perceived roughness and unrefined nature. Introduced to her role as prioress of an impoverished abbey in England, Marie’s journey unfolds under the weight of starvation, disease, and the lingering echoes of her tumultuous past. This novel, Groff’s first since her acclaimed work "Fates and Furies", serves as a powerful exploration of female agency, creativity, and spiritual fervor, wrapped in a visceral portrait of life at the fringes of society.

Initially daunted by her new environment, Marie soon discovers a profound sense of belonging with her unique sisters. She channels her past desires into a commitment to nurture her newfound family and a determination to lead. As she grapples with her identity against the encroaching chaos of the world around her, her visions of divine inspiration and leadership become her stronghold.

"Matrix" intricately weaves the sacred with the profane, displaying the violence and ecstasy that accompany an existence committed to belief and passion.
