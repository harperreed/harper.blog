This remarkable anthology reprints Hugo winner Vinge's "True Names" (1981), the story that began SF's cyberpunk revolution, with 11 essays showing its effect on science as well as fiction. <br /><br />Here is a feast of articles by computer scientists and journalists on the cutting edge of the field, writing about innovations and developments of the Internet.<br /><br />____Including, among others:__<br />* Danny Hillis: Founder of thinking machines and the first Disney Fellow.<br /><br />* Timothy C. May: former chief scientist at Intel--a major insider in the field of computers and technology.<br /><br />* Marvin Minsky: Cofounder of the MIT Artificial Intelligence Lab.<br /><br />* Chip Morningstar and F. Randall Farmer: Codevelopers of habitat, the first real computer interactive environment.<br /><br />* Mark Pesce: Cocreator of VRML and the author of the <i>Playful World: How Technology Transforms Our Imagination</i>.<br /><br />* Richard M. Stallman: Research affiliate with MIT; the founder of the Free Software Movement.
